## Overview

13 students at Skill Hub assembled a working automatic hand dispenser using an IR sensor, transistor, and DC pump — connecting sensor-based automation to a real hygiene application.

<!-- more -->

## Participants

- 13 students
- Venue — Skill Hub

## Topics

- Sensor-based automation concepts
- IR sensor for motion/hand detection
- Transistor as a switch to control a DC pump

## Activities

- Assembled circuit using IR sensor, transistor, DC pump, and power source
- Tested the dispenser by detecting hand movement
- Troubleshot and refined circuit connections
- Observed how the sensor triggered the pump automatically

## Highlights

- Students explained the role of the sensor and transistor in controlling the system
- Seeing the pump activate from a hand wave was an exciting moment
- Strong problem-solving and collaboration during assembly
- Directly linked to everyday hygiene and safety applications
